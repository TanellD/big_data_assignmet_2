upright=1.35|thumb|GAZ Chaika – American styled with imposing front closely resembling 1955-56 Packards upright=1.35|thumb|GAZ-13 Chaika limousine side / rear The GAZ Chaika (), which means gull, is a luxury automobile from the Soviet Union made by GAZ (Gorkovsky Avtomobilny Zavod, translated as Gorky Automobile Plant (Russian: ГАЗ or Го́рьковский автомоби́льный заво́д)). The vehicle is one step down from the ZIL-111 limousine, and was produced in much larger numbers. Chaika production consisted of two generations, the GAZ-13 from 1959 to 1981 and the GAZ-14 from 1977 to 1988. == GAZ-13 Chaika == The GAZ-13 ChaikaАвтомобиль Чайка. Руководство по эксплуатации. The GAZ-13 was powered by a 195-hp SAE gross 5.5 L V8 and driven through a push-button automatic transmission of a similar design to the Chrysler TorqueFlite unit. It was offered as a saloon (GAZ-13), limousine (GAZ-13A), and four-door cabriolet (GAZ-13B) with an electrohydraulic top. The cabriolet was made in 1961 and 1962 for official parades. A station wagon version was also offered as a hearse or ambulance. == GAZ-14 Chaika == The GAZ-13's styling had become antequated by the 1970s, and it was succeeded by the more modern GAZ-14 ChaikaГАЗ-14 «Чайка».
